About the Pardina de Broto and her new inhabitants

Nestled amidst the breathtaking landscapes of the Huesca Pyrenees, Broto is a picturesque village that exudes a rustic charm and a rich cultural heritage. With its cobblestone streets, traditional stone houses, and a stunning backdrop of the Ordesa y Monte Perdido National Park, Broto offers a tranquil escape into the heart of the Spanish Pyrenees.

The Pardinas de Broto are a series of small, traditional villages located on the outskirts of Broto, now uninhabited, and are used to take care of the livestock, depending whether the family that owns the lands still continue with the tradition. Each Pardina has its own unique character and charm, some still with cobblestone streets and traditional stone houses (now falling down from not being used), where you enjoy the tranquility of the environment and the immensity of nature and its great mountains.
